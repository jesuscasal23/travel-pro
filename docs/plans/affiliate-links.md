# Fichi — Affiliate Program Research

> Last updated: February 2026. Commission rates and program terms change
> periodically; verify current terms directly with each partner before
> signing contracts.

---

## Overview

Fichi monetises through three affiliate partnerships:

| Partner      | Category   | Commission model                         |
| ------------ | ---------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Skyscanner   | Flights    | Revenue share (% of Skyscanner's margin) |
| Booking.com  | Hotels     | % of completed stay value                |
| GetYourGuide | Activities | % of activity booking value              |

None of these programs require you to handle payments, issue tickets, or
support customers. Your only job is to send qualified traffic via a tracked
link. The partner handles everything after the click.

---

## 1. Skyscanner

### How it works

Skyscanner is a **flight meta-search engine** — it does not sell flights
itself. Instead, it sends users to airlines and OTAs (online travel agencies)
and earns a referral fee from them. As an affiliate, you earn a percentage of
that referral fee, not a percentage of the flight price.

This means the per-click payouts are small in absolute terms:

| Product  | Typical payout per click-out |
| -------- | ---------------------------- |
| Flights  | £0.07 – £0.30                |
| Car hire | £0.30 – £0.40                |
| Hotels   | ~£1.00                       |

The headline "up to 20% commission" refers to 20% of Skyscanner's revenue on
a referral, not 20% of the ticket price. A €500 flight might earn Skyscanner
€5 in referral fees; your 20% cut is €1.

### Cookie duration

30 days (industry standard). If the user clicks your link and books within 30
days, you earn the commission.

### How to join

1. Apply via **Impact Radius** (impact.com) — Skyscanner's affiliate network
2. Submit your website URL and traffic details
3. Approval takes 2–5 business days
4. Once approved, get tracking links from Impact dashboard

### Verification / requirements

Skyscanner has a **manual review process**. Requirements:

- Minimum **5,000 unique visitors/month** on your website
- Travel-relevant content (not just any niche)
- HTTPS enabled
- Functional site with good UX
- Influencers with **1,000+ followers** on relevant platforms can also apply

There is no fee to join. Skyscanner does not accept pure social-media-only
accounts without a website.

### Payout

- Managed through **Impact Radius**
- Payments issued approximately **30 days after the end of the invoiced month**
- Payment methods: bank transfer, PayPal (varies by region)
- No publicly stated minimum payout threshold (Impact handles this; typically ~$10)

### Affiliate portal

[partners.skyscanner.net](https://www.partners.skyscanner.net/product/affiliates)

## 2. Booking.com

### How it works

Booking.com pays commission on **completed stays** (the guest checks out). If
a user books a hotel through your link but cancels, you earn nothing. This is
the standard model for hotel affiliates.

Commission rates:

| Product                         | Commission                                 |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Accommodation (completed stays) | **4%** of booking value                    |
| Car rentals                     | **6%** of booking value                    |
| Flights                         | **€2 / £2 flat fee** per completed booking |

For context: a €200/night hotel stay for 5 nights = €1,000 booking value →
**€40 commission**. A €600 return flight → **€2 flat fee**.

High-volume travel agents can unlock a tiered commission structure (25–40% of
Booking.com's own margin) through a separate travel agent program, but this is
not the standard affiliate program.

### Cookie duration

**Session-based by default** — the booking must be completed in the same
browser session as the click. Some integrations through Awin offer a 30-day
cookie. This is an important limitation: if a user clicks your link, thinks
about it, and books the next day from a fresh tab, you may not be credited.

### How to join

Two routes:

1. **Direct**: [booking.com/affiliate-program](https://www.booking.com/affiliate-program/v2/index.html) — self-service signup
2. **Via Awin**: Booking.com also runs its program through the Awin network, which gives access to better reporting tools

No traffic minimum is stated for the standard program. Booking.com accepts
travel bloggers, app developers, and website owners. **Social-media-only
accounts are not accepted** — you need a website.

### Verification / requirements

- Must have an active website or blog
- No explicit minimum traffic requirement (unlike Skyscanner)
- Application is reviewed manually; approval typically within a few days
- Geographic restrictions: program availability varies by country

### Payout

- Monthly payments
- **€100 minimum payout threshold** — earnings accumulate until you hit €100,
  then payment is issued within 2–3 weeks of receiving your credit slip
- Payment methods: bank transfer, PayPal (varies by region)

### Affiliate portal

[affiliate.booking.com](https://www.booking.com/affiliate-program/v2/index.html)

## 3. GetYourGuide

### How it works

GetYourGuide sells **tours, activities, and experiences** (museum tickets,
cooking classes, day trips, etc.). You earn a percentage of every booking
made through your link.

Commission rates:

| Tier                      | Rate                                       |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Base (all new partners)   | **8%** of booking value                    |
| Advanced / top performers | Custom rate (negotiated, typically 10–12%) |

For context: a €100 day-trip booking → **€8 commission** at base rate.

### Cookie duration

**30 days** — if a user clicks your link and books any GetYourGuide activity
within 30 days (not just what you linked to), you earn the commission on the
entire order.

### How to join

1. Apply at [partner.getyourguide.com](https://partner.getyourguide.com)
2. Self-service signup — approval is generally fast (same day to a few days)
3. Much lower barrier to entry than Skyscanner; no stated traffic minimum

### Verification / requirements

- Open to creators, publishers, app developers, and website owners
- Available in **180+ countries**
- No public minimum traffic requirement
- GetYourGuide tends to be the most accessible of the three programs for
  early-stage products

### Payout

- **Monthly payments via PayPal** — no minimum payout threshold for PayPal
- Other payment methods may have a threshold
- Payments issued on a monthly cycle

### Affiliate portal

[partner.getyourguide.com](https://partner.getyourguide.com)

## Realistic Earnings Expectations

### Industry benchmarks

| Metric                                             | Typical range           |
| -------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Average affiliate earnings per 1,000 visitors      | ~$150 across all niches |
| Travel affiliate conversion rate (click → booking) | 1–3%                    |
| Top-performing travel affiliates (monthly)         | $3,000–$5,000+          |
| Early-stage site / app (monthly)                   | $50–$300                |

### Fichi's advantage

Standard travel affiliate sites (blogs, comparison pages) earn from cold
traffic — visitors who might not be planning a trip at all. Fichi users
are **highly qualified**: they have just built a 22-day itinerary, seen the
budget breakdown, and are actively looking at specific routes and dates.

This is among the highest purchase-intent traffic you can send to an affiliate
partner. Expected conversion rates should be **above the 1–3% baseline**.

### Rough projections by scale

Assumptions: 2 travelers per trip, 1 hotel booking per 5 cities, 2 activities
per city, average hotel stay €150/night × 3 nights per city.

| Monthly active users generating itineraries | Estimated affiliate revenue |
| ------------------------------------------- | --------------------------- |
| 100 users                                   | €40–€150/month              |
| 500 users                                   | €200–€750/month             |
| 2,000 users                                 | €800–€3,000/month           |
| 10,000 users                                | €4,000–€15,000/month        |

The wide range reflects uncertainty in conversion rates and booking values.
Hotels (Booking.com at 4%) will be the largest revenue driver by value since
hotel bookings are high-ticket. Activities (GetYourGuide at 8%) have the best
rate but lower booking values. Flights (Skyscanner flat fee) contribute the
least per booking despite being the most prominent.

### Which program to prioritise

1. **Booking.com first** — highest revenue per converted user due to hotel
   booking value × 4% rate. A 5-city trip with €600 in hotels = €24
   commission per booking.
2. **GetYourGuide second** — 8% rate and 30-day cookie make it attractive;
   easy to get approved early.
3. **Skyscanner third** — low per-click value, but our contextual deep links
   (pre-filled route + dates from Amadeus optimization) give us an advantage.
   Still worth having for completeness and user trust.

## Signup Checklist

Before applying, you need:

- [ ] A live, publicly accessible website (the app itself qualifies once deployed)
- [ ] HTTPS enabled (already done — Vercel handles this)
- [ ] A privacy policy page mentioning affiliate relationships (already built at `/privacy`)
- [ ] Some real traffic or user activity to show (Skyscanner specifically)
- [ ] A registered business or individual tax details for payout setup

Order of signup recommended: **GetYourGuide → Booking.com → Skyscanner**
(easiest approval first, build a track record, then apply to Skyscanner once
you have traffic data to show).

## Current Implementation Status

The affiliate infrastructure is already built in the codebase:

| File                                         | What it does                                                        |
| -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `src/lib/affiliate/link-generator.ts`        | Builds tracked deep links for all three partners                    |
| `src/app/api/v1/affiliate/redirect/route.ts` | Internal redirect endpoint that logs clicks to DB before forwarding |
| `src/app/trip/[id]/summary/page.tsx`         | Shows per-leg flight links + hotel/activity booking cards           |

The affiliate IDs currently in the code are **placeholders**
(`TRAVEL_PRO_AID`, `TRAVEL_PRO_ID`). Replace these with real IDs from each
partner dashboard once approved:

```
# .env.local (and Vercel env vars)
BOOKING_AFFILIATE_ID=<your real Booking.com AID>
GYG_PARTNER_ID=<your real GetYourGuide partner ID>
```

Skyscanner's tracking is handled via the `ref=travel-pro` query parameter in
`buildFlightLink()` — update the `AFFILIATE_IDS.skyscanner` value in
`link-generator.ts` once you have your Impact publisher ID.
